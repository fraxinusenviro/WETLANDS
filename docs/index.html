Hydric Soil Reference Guide (NS Adapted)

Adapted from Northeastern US Hydric Soil Indicators with Probable Application in Nova Scotia (2012).

All Soils (A Indicators)

  • A1 Histosol: Organic soil with at least 40 cm thickness (also includes wetland organic soils over bedrock/fragmental material with organic-filled interstices).
  • A2 Histic Epipedon: Organic material 20–40 cm thick over mineral soil with chroma ≤2; requires supporting aquic evidence.
  • A4 Hydrogen Sulfide: Rotten egg odour detected within 30 cm of surface.
  • A5 Stratified Layers: Stratified layers beginning within 15 cm with low value/chroma and organic requirements for sandy layers.
  • A11 Depleted Below Dark Surface: Depleted/gleyed matrix (≥60% chroma ≤2) within 30 cm plus strict thickness/overlying color criteria.
  • A12 Thick Dark Surface: Depleted/gleyed matrix starts at or below 30 cm with dark low-chroma overlying layers.

Sandy Soils (S Indicators)

  • S1 Sandy Mucky Mineral: Mucky modified sandy layer ≥5 cm starting within 15 cm.
  • S4 Sandy Gleyed Matrix: Gleyed matrix occupies ≥60% of a layer starting within 15 cm.
  • S5 Sandy Redox: Layer within 15 cm with matrix ≥60% chroma ≤2 and qualifying redox concentrations.
  • S6 Stripped Matrix: Iron/organic stripped and translocated splotchy pattern, stripped zones ≥10% volume.
  • S7 Dark Surface: Dark low-chroma layer 10 cm thick within 15 cm with ≥70% coated sand grains.
  • S8 Polyvalue Below Surface: Dark layer within 15 cm with polyvalue pattern below to 30 cm/spodic horizon.
  • S9 Thin Dark Surface: Dark layer ≥5 cm within 15 cm, underlain by low-chroma material to 30 cm/spodic horizon.

Loamy / Clayey Soils (F Indicators)

  • F2 Loamy Gleyed Matrix: Gleyed matrix ≥60% in layer beginning within 30 cm.
  • F3 Depleted Matrix: Depleted matrix ≥60% chroma ≤2 with required depth/thickness combinations.
  • F6 Redox Dark Surface: Dark mineral layer in upper 30 cm with required redox concentration thresholds.
  • F7 Depleted Dark Surface: Dark surface with qualifying redox depletions and matrix conditions.
  • F8 Redox Depressions: In closed depressions, ≥5% distinct/prominent redox concentrations in upper 15 cm.

Problem Soil Indicators (Conditional Use)

  • F21 Red Parent Material
  • TF12 Very Shallow Dark Surface
  • S3 5 cm Mucky Peat or Peat
  • F12 Iron/Manganese Masses

Use conditional indicators where standard indicators fail but hydrology and hydrophytic vegetation support hydric interpretation.

Using WETLANDS NTB

  • Use Launch New Form to start, or Drafts Library to resume prior work.
  • The form autosaves while you type. Returning home prompts to save a resumable snapshot.
  • In soil horizons, enter Munsell codes (e.g., 10YR 4/3) for Matrix/Redox fields.
  • Known Munsell codes show as Code (Description) and PDF exports include these descriptions.
  • Submit when complete, then manage exports in Submitted Forms.
